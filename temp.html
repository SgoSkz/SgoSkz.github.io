Buddhism & Confucianism

Synopsis

Confucianism and Buddhism had many parallels in their origins and similar patterns when they were changed. Buddhism was considered to be more non-secular than Confucianism, later due to its non-secular ideas, Buddhism was considered to be a Religion rather than Philosophy. Of course, the fact that Buddhism and Confucianism had many parallels does not reflect their political choices and power.

Definitions

Religion & Philosophy

Religion and Philosophy are widely paralleled, the overlap between both Religion and Philosophy make it hard to define each one absolutely. During the time, Buddhism was considered by many to be Religious when it first came to Ancient China. Confucianism was considered to a Philosophy, of course, some people during the time disagreed and considered Confucius to be a religious figure. Putting an absolute definition of Religion or Philosophy on either Buddhism and/or Confucianism could be argued to be either Religion or Philosophy due to the loose definitions of Religion and Philosophy and their many overlaps.
